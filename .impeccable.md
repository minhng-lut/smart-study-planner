## Design Context

### Users
Students using a web app to organize academic work. They need to register, log in, create courses, break courses into tasks with deadlines and estimated hours, mark progress, and understand study pressure before falling behind.

### Brand Personality
Calm, disciplined, smart. The interface should feel like a playful student planner rather than a corporate dashboard. It should reduce stress, create clarity, and make planning feel achievable.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial planner with warm paper-like surfaces, structured layouts, and subtle playful accents. Support both light and dark themes. Favor a study-journal feel over generic SaaS or neon tech aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. Make planning feel clear, not heavy.
2. Use strong hierarchy so students can read the page quickly under time pressure.
3. Keep the tone warm and intelligent, avoiding sterile enterprise styling.
4. Let visual details reinforce study and scheduling, not abstract tech vibes.
5. Design every screen to feel intentional enough that it could be a real product, not scaffolding.
